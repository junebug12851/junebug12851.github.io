### 2026-07-13 — Maintenance round-up: PSE2 0.24.4, RAP 2.60.1, Games 0.22.0, Stories 0.3.4; the 12th blogged (0.15.10)

A round-up pass. All four references fast-forwarded cleanly (no force-pushes), and every
commit on all four sat on **2026-07-12** — one complete day, so one day post.

**The day's theme, unplanned and shared: the oracle.** Pokered Save Editor 2 had its single
biggest day yet (`0.16.6` → `0.24.4-alpha`), and the whole of it is one idea — hand your work
to something outside yourself and let it grade you. The map screen stopped drawing a *picture*
of the map and started running the game's own overworld pipeline; a legally backed-up cartridge,
booted headless in an emulator, then became the judge and compared every buffer byte for byte.
It said **no** about the border ring, which turned out to be the neighbouring maps bleeding in
rather than a wall of border block; connection strips closed the gap (78/78 verified against the
ROM's own compiled structs). The six "glitch" palettes turned out to be a pointer *subtracted*
from a table of eight fade palettes, so anything not a multiple of three straddles two entries —
exactly four contrast levels and exactly six glitch palettes, and the console confirmed all ten.
Then a **Game Boy sound chip and Gen 1's sequencer, written in C++**, with the cartridge's own
256 bytes of engine state photographed *every frame* as the parity oracle — 46 tracks byte for
byte. It caught three bugs of *tidiness* (the port had done the right thing where the game does
the wrong one) and, most usefully, caught the **oracle itself** being wrong. The glitch music
turned out not to exist: song ids are computed from header addresses, so a three-channel song
occupies three ids and the other two are its own inner voices — 46 tracks + **105 inner voices**,
zero garbage. And working out what the map *means* uncovered a save-corrupting bug that had been
shipping for years: the collision lists are shared in the cartridge, the importer walked the chain
one step out of phase, and the editor *writes* that pointer — so a Poké Mart got a bedroom's walls.

Random AI Prompt (`2.58.0` → `2.60.1`) spent the same day on the same idea from the other side:
it put its Android build on a **real Android runtime** and measured the 1,000-prompt promise it
had been asserting (253 ms to render, memory flat as the list grows 50×) — and found that the
"defect" the gate first reported was **the test's own** (results accumulate across rolls, so a
test waiting for "200 generated" after a 20-prompt baseline waits forever, then reports its own
timeout as the app being slow). It closed with a **critical command injection** CodeQL found on
the release PR: a filename could close the quote in a shell string and run the rest. Fixed
structurally — no shell at all.

Fairy Fox Games planted **Brim** (`0.22.0`), the 13th game and a new verb (pour), whose whole hook
is a delay line: the stream keeps arriving after you close the spout, so you must stop *early by
exactly the amount still in the air*. Skyline got the wind (`0.21.1`). Fairy Fox Stories ran a pure
grow day (`0.3.4`) and **held** its plant rather than planting early, because its own three-day
cadence gate said it wasn't due.
